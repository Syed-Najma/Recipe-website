Kashmiri wazwaan Rista
Non-Vegetarian | Dinner | Lunch
Ingredients
For the meatballs (Rista):
  • 500 g mutton (boneless, from leg or shoulder)
  • 2 tbsp mutton fat (optional, for softness)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder (for mild heat and colour)
  • 1 tsp fennel powder (saunf)
  • ½ tsp dry ginger powder (shonth)
  • 1 tbsp ghee (for binding)
    • For the gravy:
  • 3 tbsp mustard oil or ghee
  • 1 bay leaf (tej patta)
  • 2–3 cloves (laung)
  • 2 green cardamoms (elaichi)
  • 1 black cardamom (badi elaichi)
  • 1-inch piece cinnamon (dalchini)
  • ½ tsp fennel powder
  • ½ tsp dry ginger powder
  • 2 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder (for rich red colour)
  • 1 pinch asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
    • For the red colour (optional traditional method):
  • 1 tsp ratanjog (alkanet root) soaked in warm water or fried in ghee, then strained (for authentic red hue)
    • Method
  • Prepare the meat paste

    -Traditionally, mutton is pounded on a stone slab (pashan) until it becomes a smooth, stretchy paste.

    -Alternatively, use a food processor: grind mutton (and fat if using) until smooth and sticky.

    -Add salt, chili powder, fennel powder, dry ginger powder, and ghee.

    -Knead well for 8–10 minutes until the mixture is elastic and binds easily.

  • Shape the Rista (meatballs)

    -Grease your palms with a little ghee.

    -Take small portions and roll into smooth round balls (about the size of a walnut).

    -Cover and set aside.

  • Prepare the gravy base

    -Heat mustard oil in a deep pan until smoking point; lower flame.

    -Add bay leaf, cloves, cardamoms, cinnamon, and a pinch of hing. Sauté for a few seconds.

    -Add fennel powder, dry ginger powder, and Kashmiri red chili powder.

    -Quickly add 2 cups of water to prevent the spices from burning.

  • Cook the Ristas

    -Gently drop the meatballs into the simmering gravy one by one.

    -Do not stir immediately — let them set for 3–4 minutes, then swirl the pan gently.

    -Cover and cook on low flame for 30–35 minutes until the meatballs are tender and gravy thickens.

  • Add the red hue

    -If using ratanjog, stir in the coloured ghee or strained water now for the signature deep red tone.

    • Serving

    Serve hot with Rice or Naan

    Rista is a royal delicacy — soft, spongy red mutton balls in a silky aromatic gravy — representing the skill and tradition of kashmiri cuisine.
