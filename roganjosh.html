Kashmiri Mutton Rogan Josh
Non-Vegetarian | Dinner | Lunch
Ingredients
For the mutton curry:
  • 750 g mutton (bone-in pieces preferred)
  • 3 tbsp mustard oil (or ghee for richer taste)
  • 4–5 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2–3 green cardamoms (elaichi)
  • 1 black cardamom (badi elaichi)
  • 2–3 cloves (laung)
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick (dalchini)
  • 1 bay leaf (tej patta)
  • 1 tsp fennel powder (saunf)
  • 1 tsp dry ginger powder (shonth)
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder (for colour and mild heat)
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 pinch asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 tbsp beaten curd (yogurt)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water (adjust as needed)
    • Method
  • Heat oil and temper spices

    -Heat mustard oil until smoking point, then lower the heat.

    -Add cinnamon, cloves, cardamoms, bay leaf, and a pinch of hing. Sauté until aromatic.

  • Sear the mutton

    -Add mutton pieces and fry on medium-high flame until they get a nice brown colour on all sides.

    -Add crushed garlic and sauté for another minute.

  • Add the dry spices

    -Lower heat and add turmeric, red chili powder, fennel powder, and dry ginger powder. Mix quickly so spices don’t burn.

  • Add yogurt and simmer

    -Stir in the beaten curd slowly while stirring to prevent curdling.

    -Add salt and pour 2 cups of water. Mix well and bring to a boil.

  • Slow cook for rich flavour

    -Cover and simmer on low heat for 60–75 minutes until mutton is tender and oil (rogan) floats on top.

    -Stir occasionally and add a bit more water if needed to maintain gravy consistency.

    • Serving

    Serve hot with Kashmiri steamed rice or Sheermal.

    This classic Kashmiri dish is rich, aromatic, and deeply spiced without using onions or tomatoes—true to its traditional roots.
