Kashir Gaad – Authentic Traditional Kashmiri Fish Recipe
Non-Vegetarian | Dinner | Lunch
Ingredients
For the fish (Gaade):
  • 500 g river fish (cleaned and cut into pieces)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Mustard oil for frying (traditional, gives authentic flavour)
    • For the vegetables:
  • 1 cup nadru (lotus stem), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup muji (radish), peeled and cut into chunks
  • 2 cups saag (Kashmiri haak greens or spinach), roughly chopped
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tsp fennel seed powder (saunf)
  • ½ tsp dry ginger powder (shonth)
  • ½ tsp Kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 green chilies (optional)
  • Salt to taste
    • Optional Tempering:
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • 1–2 dried red chilies
    • Method
  • Marinate and fry the fish

    -Rub fish pieces with salt and turmeric.

    -Heat mustard oil until it reaches smoking point, then reduce the flame and fry fish until golden on all sides. Remove and set aside.

  • Cook nadru & muji

    -In the same oil, add lotus stem and radish pieces. Sauté for 2–3 minutes.

    -Add turmeric, fennel powder, dry ginger powder, chili powder, salt, and crushed garlic. Mix well.

    -Pour in about 2 cups of water. Cover and simmer until nadru and muji are almost tender.

  • Add the greens

    -Add chopped saag (haak). Stir and cook until wilted.

    -If needed, add more water to make a light gravy.

  • Add the fried fish

    -Gently slide in the fried fish pieces. Let it simmer on low heat for 8–10 minutes so the fish absorbs the flavours.

  • Optional tempering (tadka)

    -Heat 1 tsp oil, splutter cumin seeds and dried red chilies, then pour over the curry for aroma.

    • Serving

    Serve hot with Kashmiri steamed rice. This is a soulful winter-style dish from the Kashmiri cuisine.
