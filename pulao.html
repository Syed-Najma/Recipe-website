Kashmiri Pulao
Non-Vegetarian | Dinner | Lunch
Ingredients
For the rice:
  • 1½ cups basmati rice (long-grain, aromatic)
  • 3 cups water
  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 bay leaf (tej patta)
  • 2–3 green cardamoms (elaichi)
  • 1 black cardamom (badi elaichi)
  • 2–3 cloves (laung)
  • 1 small piece of cinnamon stick (dalchini)
  • Salt to taste
    • For the flavouring:
  • ½ cup milk
  • A few strands of saffron (kesar)
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional, for slight sweetness)
    • For the garnish:
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 2 tbsp cashews
  • 2 tbsp almonds (blanched or sliced)
  • 2 tbsp raisins
  • 2 tbsp chopped dried apricots (optional)
  • Fresh pomegranate seeds (for topping)
  • 2–3 tbsp chopped apple, pineapple, or pear (traditional Kashmiri fruit garnish)
    • Method
  • Rinse and soak rice

    -Wash basmati rice 2–3 times until the water runs clear.

    -Soak it for about 20 minutes, then drain completely.

  • Infuse saffron milk

    -Warm ½ cup milk and add saffron strands. Let it sit to release the golden colour and aroma.

  • Fry whole spices

    -Heat 2 tbsp ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan.

    -Add bay leaf, cardamoms, cloves, and cinnamon. Sauté until fragrant.

  • Cook the rice

    -Add soaked rice and stir gently for 1–2 minutes so the grains are coated in ghee.

    -Pour in 3 cups water and add salt.

    -Cover and cook on low flame until the rice is 90% done.

  • Add saffron milk and sweetness

    -Gently pour the saffron milk and sugar over the rice.

    -Mix lightly and let it steam on very low heat (dum) for 5–7 minutes until fluffy.

  • Prepare the dry fruit garnish

    -In a small pan, heat 2 tbsp ghee.

    -Fry cashews, almonds, raisins, and apricots until golden. Remove and set aside.

  • Assemble the pulao

    -Once rice is done, transfer it to a serving dish.

    -Garnish with fried nuts, raisins, chopped fruits, and pomegranate seeds.

    • Serving

    Serve hot.

    This aromatic dish blends the fragrance of spices with the sweetness of fruits and nuts — a true royal delicacy symbolizing Kashmiri hospitality and balance of flavours.
