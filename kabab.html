Kashmiri Seekh Kabab
Non-Vegetarian | Dinner | Lunch
Ingredients
For the kabab mixture:
  • 500 g minced mutton (preferably from the leg, finely ground — almost paste-like)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped (optional in authentic Wazwan — traditional version uses no onion)
  • 1 tsp fennel powder (saunf)
  • ½ tsp dry ginger powder (shonth)
  • ½ tsp cumin powder (jeera)
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder
  • ½ tsp black pepper powder
  • 2–3 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp gram flour (besan, lightly roasted)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil (to keep mixture moist)
    • For shaping and grilling:
  • Ghee or oil for basting
  • Metal or wooden skewers
    • Method
  • Prepare the mince

    -Wash and drain mutton mince well. It should be very fine — almost smooth — for traditional **** texture.

    -If needed, pulse it once in a grinder for 2–3 seconds to get a paste-like consistency.

  • Mix the spices

    -In a large bowl, combine mince with fennel powder, dry ginger powder, cumin powder, chili powder, black pepper, crushed garlic, and grated ginger.

    -Add salt and roasted gram flour.

    -Drizzle a bit of ghee or oil and knead the mixture well using your hand for 5–7 minutes until it becomes sticky and smooth.

    -This kneading is key — it binds the kababs naturally without egg or breadcrumbs.

  • Shape onto skewers

    -Wet your palms slightly.

    -Take a small portion of the mixture and press it evenly around a skewer, forming a long cylindrical shape (about 5–6 inches).

    -Repeat with remaining mixture.

  • Cook the seekh kababs

    -Option 1 (Traditional charcoal method): Grill over hot coals, rotating occasionally, until evenly browned.

    -Option 2 (Oven): Preheat oven to 200°C. Place kababs on a greased tray and bake for 12–15 minutes, flipping halfway through.

    -Option 3 (Tawa/Pan): Heat a non-stick pan, drizzle ghee, and cook kababs on medium heat until golden on all sides.

  • Baste for flavour

    -Brush kababs with ghee or oil while cooking to keep them juicy and enhance smoky aroma.

    • Serving

    Serve hot with Rice or Naan

    These seekh kababs are smoky, juicy, and mildly spiced — showcasing the balance of flavours that define kashmiri cuisine.
