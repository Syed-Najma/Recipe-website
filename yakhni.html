Kashmiri Mutton Yakhni
Non-Vegetarian | Dinner | Lunch
Ingredients
For the yakhni (curd-based gravy):
  • 750 g mutton (bone-in pieces preferred)
  • 1 cup thick curd (yogurt), whisked
  • 3 tbsp mustard oil or ghee
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 bay leaf (tej patta)
  • 2–3 green cardamoms (elaichi)
  • 1 black cardamom (badi elaichi)
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick (dalchini)
  • 2–3 cloves (laung)
  • 1 tsp fennel powder (saunf)
  • ½ tsp dry ginger powder (shonth)
  • 1 pinch asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water (or as required)
    • Method
  • Prepare the mutton base

    -Heat mustard oil until smoking point, then reduce flame.

    -Add bay leaf, cinnamon, cardamoms, cloves, and hing. Sauté for a few seconds until aromatic.

  • Add mutton pieces

    -Add mutton and fry until light brown from all sides.

    -Add crushed garlic and stir for 1–2 minutes to infuse flavor.

  • Add powdered spices

    -Lower the heat and add fennel powder, dry ginger powder, and salt. Mix gently.

    -Pour in 2 cups of water and let the mutton simmer until it becomes tender (about 40–50 minutes on low flame).

  • Prepare curd mixture

    -In a bowl, whisk curd well until smooth. Add 2–3 tablespoons of the hot broth from the pan to temper it.

    -Slowly pour the curd into the mutton while stirring continuously to prevent curdling.

  • Simmer the yakhni

    -Keep stirring for a few minutes until the yogurt blends into the broth and a creamy, fragrant gravy forms.

    -Simmer gently on low heat for 10–15 minutes until oil (rogan) begins to float on top.

    • Serving

    Serve hot with Rice.

    This is a traditional Kashmiri yogurt-based mutton curry. It’s part of Wazwan, the royal multi-course Kashmiri feast. Unlike many Indian curries, it does not use onion, tomato, or heavy spices — instead, it gets its rich, creamy taste from curd (yogurt) and its fragrance from fennel (saunf), dry ginger (shonth), and cardamoms.
