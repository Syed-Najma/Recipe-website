Kashmiri Wazwan Aab Gosh
Non-Vegetarian | Dinner | Lunch
Ingredients
For the mutton base:
  • 750 g mutton (preferably bone-in pieces from the leg or shoulder)
  • 2 tbsp ghee or mustard oil
  • 3–4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2–3 cloves (laung)
  • 2–3 green cardamoms (elaichi)
  • 1 black cardamom (badi elaichi)
  • 2–3 cloves (laung)
  • 1 small piece cinnamon (dalchini)
  • 1 bay leaf (tej patta)
  • Salt to taste
    • For the milk gravy:
  • 2 cups full-fat milk
  • ½ cup thick curd (well beaten)
  • 1 tbsp gram flour (besan, lightly roasted)
  • 1 tsp fennel powder (saunf)
  • ½ tsp dry ginger powder (shonth)
  • ½ tsp black pepper powder
  • 1 pinch asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 tbsp ghee (for finishing)
    • Method
  • Prepare the mutton stock

    -Heat ghee or mustard oil in a deep pan until hot.

    Add bay leaf, cloves, cinnamon, cardamoms, and a pinch of hing. Sauté until fragrant.

    -Add crushed garlic and mutton pieces.

    -Sear on medium heat until lightly browned on all sides.

    -Add salt and about 1 cup water.

    -Cover and simmer until meat turns soft and tender (about 40–50 minutes).

  • Prepare the milk-curd mixture

    -In a bowl, whisk together milk, curd, and roasted gram flour until smooth (this prevents curdling).

    -Add fennel powder, dry ginger powder, and black pepper powder. Mix well.

  • Combine stock and milk mixture

    -Lower the flame and slowly pour the milk-curd mixture into the cooked mutton stock, stirring continuously.

    -Keep the flame low — never boil the milk mixture, or it may curdle.

  • Simmer gently

    -Cook on low flame for 10–15 minutes, stirring gently, until the gravy turns creamy and coats the meat well.

    -Check salt and adjust if needed.

  • Finish with ghee

    -Heat 2 tbsp ghee separately and pour it over the curry.

    -Cover and let it rest for 5 minutes for the flavours to meld.

    • Serving

    Serve hot with rice

    This dish (literally “meat in milk”) is a mild, creamy dish — symbolizing the balance of richness and subtlety that defines the kashmiri tradition.
